## Slide 3: Revenue Growth & Unit Economics
Our T2D3 trajectory shows consistent execution: we've doubled revenue year-over-year for the past three years, reaching $30M ARR. 
Our NDR of 118% exceeds the median 104% benchmark for companies in our previous bracket, achieved through systematic expansion. CAC payback is 14 months, improving from 18 months a year ago, with our New CAC Ratio of $1.80 to acquire $1.00 of New Customer ARR better than the median $2.00.
 LTV/CAC ratio is 4.2x, indicating healthy unit economics that support sustainable growth at scale.

## Slide 4: Market Size & Opportunity

The vertical SaaS market we serve represents a $12B TAM, growing at 22% annually. Vertical SaaS reached an estimated $157 billion in 2025, representing approximately 35% of the total $450 billion SaaS market, and is growing at 18-22% CAGR versus 12-15% for horizontal SaaS.
 Our specific vertical—healthcare workflow automation—is particularly underserved, with most providers still using horizontal tools that require extensive customization. We're capturing 0.25% of this market today, with clear line of sight to 2% market share by 2028.

## Slide 5: Competitive Landscape
We face three primary competitors with distinct strategic positions. MedFlow dominates the mid-market with a self-serve motion and $200M ARR, but their generic workflow engine struggles with complex multi-provider scenarios. **Response to MedFlow**: Targeting complex multi-provider scenarios where their generic engine fails, leveraging our purpose-built compliance workflows. ClinicalOps targets enterprise customers with deep customization but suffers from 18-month implementation cycles and 85% gross retention. NewEntrant raised $100M last year and is aggressively underpricing by 40% in our core segment. **Response to NewEntrant's pricing**: Accelerating enterprise focus where price sensitivity is lower and our compliance workflows create higher switching costs.

## Slide 6: Product Roadmap 2026

Our product strategy focuses on three vectors: AI-powered workflow optimization, embedded financial services, and expanded specialty modules. Because these platforms accumulate industry-specific data at scale, they are uniquely positioned to train AI models that provide value no horizontal platform can match, with SaaS companies having AI deeply incorporated into their products outperforming peers across all ARR bands.
 Q1 delivers predictive scheduling AI trained on our 50M+ appointment dataset. Q2 launches embedded payments and practice lending. Q3 adds specialty modules for cardiology and orthopedics.

## Slide 7: Go-to-Market Evolution

We're evolving from product-led growth to a hybrid enterprise motion. Our PLG engine generates 60% of new logos under $50K ACV, while our enterprise team closes deals above $100K ACV with 140% NDR versus 105% for SMB. Companies with $15M-$30M+ ARR see 40% of their growth driven by expansion.
 We're investing in industry-specific sales specialists and expanding our partner channel, which currently drives 25% of enterprise deals. The goal is reaching $100M ARR with 70% coming from customers above $50K ACV.

## Slide 8: Team & Hiring Plan

Scaling from $30M to $100M ARR requires doubling our team from 180 to 350 people over 24 months. Engineering gets 40% of new hires to deliver our AI and embedded finance roadmap, sales grows from 35 to 80 reps, and Customer Success expands to maintain our industry-leading retention. The total median spend across all departments is 95% of ARR for bootstrapped companies while equity-backed are spending 107% of ARR.

For the majority of SaaS businesses with moderate growth rates, valuation multiples tend to fall within a more conservative 2x to 5x ARR range, though top-performing, high-growth SaaS firms attract venture funding at around 10x ARR.

We're raising $35M Series B at a $150M pre-money valuation, representing 8x ARR multiple consistent with high-growth vertical SaaS benchmarks. The median pre-money Series B valuation was $118.9M for primary rounds in Q3 2025, up from $102.8M in Q3 2024.
 Use of funds: 40% for product development and AI capabilities, 35% for sales and marketing, 15% for geographic expansion, 10% for working capital. This funding provides 30 months runway to reach cash flow positive operations.

## Slide 11: Key Risks & Mitigations
Our primary risks span competitive, execution, market, and regulatory dimensions. **Competitive risk**: NewEntrant's $100M war chest and 40% pricing discount threaten our core market. **Mitigation**: Accelerating AI differentiation and doubling down on enterprise accounts where price sensitivity is lower and switching costs are higher. **Execution risk**: Scaling engineering team 3x while maintaining product quality and delivery timelines. **Mitigation**: Hiring proven engineering leaders from similar-stage vertical SaaS companies and implementing robust technical review processes. **Market risk**: Healthcare budget pressures could extend sales cycles and reduce deal sizes. **Mitigation**: Demonstrating clear ROI within 12 months and offering flexible pricing models. **Regulatory risk**: Changes in healthcare data privacy regulations could require significant product modifications and delay enterprise sales cycles by 6+ months. **Mitigation**: Establishing dedicated compliance team and building regulatory change monitoring into our product development process.
