# Rubric: agi_counterargument

**Domain:** argumentation
**Total Points:** 34
**Pass Threshold:** 0.8

## Criterion 1: arg_steelman
**Category:** intellectual_honesty
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Presents a charitable interpretation of the AGI-by-2030 claim with specific supporting evidence
**Pass Condition:** First paragraph includes: (1) at least 3 specific quantified advances with numerical metrics (e.g., 'GPT-4 achieved 90th percentile on bar exam'), (2) quotes or paraphrases specific timeline predictions from at least 2 named experts with their credentials, and (3) explains both the scaling hypothesis and at least one other technical pathway (e.g., multimodal integration, reasoning architectures) supporting the timeline.

## Criterion 2: arg_counter_quality
**Category:** argumentation
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Presents multiple distinct counterarguments with specific evidence and clear reasoning
**Pass Condition:** Contains 3+ distinct counterarguments where: (1) challenges AGI definition by citing at least 2 specific capabilities (e.g., long-term planning, real-world robotics) that current systems lack with measurable examples, (2) identifies at least 2 technical bottlenecks with quantified evidence (e.g., 'training costs growing 10x annually', 'hallucination rates above 15%'), and (3) names at least 1 specific regulatory barrier or safety requirement with institutional source.

## Criterion 3: arg_nuance
**Category:** sophistication
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Acknowledges uncertainty and presents conditional reasoning rather than absolute claims
**Pass Condition:** Text includes: (1) at least 4 epistemic uncertainty markers distributed throughout, (2) identifies at least 2 concrete breakthrough scenarios with specific triggering conditions (e.g., 'if compute costs drop 100x' or 'if reasoning architectures achieve X benchmark'), and (3) explicitly acknowledges at least 1 counterargument to the author's own position.

## Criterion 4: arg_readability
**Category:** communication
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Well-structured, scannable, persuasive prose
**Pass Condition:** Clear thesis with specific stance in first paragraph. Each counterargument in separate section with descriptive subheading. Conclusion weighs evidence and states probability range or confidence level. Under 600 words with no paragraph exceeding 4 sentences.
