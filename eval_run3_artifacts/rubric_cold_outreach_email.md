# Rubric: cold_outreach_email

**Domain:** cold_outreach_email
**Total Points:** 53
**Pass Threshold:** 0.8

## Criterion 1: email_subject
**Category:** engagement
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Subject line is compelling and specific — not generic or spammy
**Pass Condition:** Subject is <60 chars, references a specific metric/milestone/pain point unique to the recipient's company (not just 'congrats on Series A'), creates urgency or curiosity through specificity. No generic frameworks like 'Re: [Company]' or 'Quick question about [Company]'.

## Criterion 2: email_opening
**Category:** engagement
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** First sentence demonstrates research and relevance to recipient
**Pass Condition:** Opens by connecting a specific recent development to a relevant insight, prediction, or pattern the sender observed. Must go beyond surface-level research to show analytical thinking about the company's trajectory or challenges.

## Criterion 3: email_value_prop
**Category:** persuasion
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Clearly articulates what the angel brings beyond capital
**Pass Condition:** Specific operational value: domain expertise, network, customer intros, hiring help. Concrete, not vague ('I can help').

## Criterion 4: email_social_proof
**Category:** credibility
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Establishes credibility without bragging
**Pass Condition:** Includes 1-2 proof points that directly parallel the recipient's business model, stage, or challenges. Generic credentials don't count - must show relevant pattern recognition or domain expertise.

## Criterion 5: email_cta
**Category:** conversion
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Call to action is low-friction and specific
**Pass Condition:** Proposes a specific, valuable conversation topic tied to the recipient's current needs/challenges. Not just 'quick call' but 'call to discuss X specific thing that would help you with Y current priority'.

## Criterion 6: email_tone
**Category:** voice
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Language avoids overly formal or sales-heavy phrasing
**Pass Condition:** Demonstrates founder-to-founder peer respect through informed observations rather than deferential language. Avoids both excessive formality AND overly casual familiarity. No qualifier phrases ('I hope', 'I think you might', 'perhaps').

## Criterion 7: email_length
**Category:** structure
**Max Points:** N/A
**Description:** Email is scannable in <30 seconds — under 150 words
**Pass Condition:** Under 100 words while maintaining all other criterion requirements. Every sentence must serve a specific purpose - no filler phrases or redundant points.
